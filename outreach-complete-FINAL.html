📖 Complete Cold Call Guide
💡 Pro Tip
This is a general framework. For CUSTOMIZED scripts based on each business's real SEO issues, click "📞 Call Script" on any lead in Search or Database tabs. AI generates unique scripts with their specific problems.
👋 OPENING (10 seconds)
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Your Company]. I'll be completely upfront — this is a cold call about your website's Google visibility, but I promise I'll keep it really quick. Is now a good time?"
💡 Why this works: Being honest immediately disarms them. Most cold callers pretend they know them. Your honesty builds instant trust. The "keep it quick" promise lowers resistance.
If they say "No, I'm busy":
"No worries at all — can I call back tomorrow morning or later this week? Takes 2 minutes, I promise."
🎯 THE HOOK (15 seconds)
"So the reason I'm calling — I was searching for plumbers in [City] on Google earlier, and [Business Name] wasn't showing up at all. I had a quick look at your site and ran it through our SEO tool. It's scoring [Score]/100, which explains why you're not appearing in searches."
💡 Why this works: You've done homework (searched for them). You have a specific number (the score). This isn't generic spam — you clearly looked at THEIR business specifically.
⚠️ THE PROBLEMS (30-40 seconds)
"There are a couple of specific things costing you calls every day. First, [explain Issue 1 in plain English with business impact]. Second, [explain Issue 2]. What this means is customers are literally finding your competitors instead of you — probably 5-10 calls a day you're missing out on."
💡 How to explain issues in plain English:

❌ Don't say: "Your H1 tag is missing"
✅ Do say: "Your site has no main heading, so Google can't figure out what you actually do"

❌ Don't say: "You need schema markup"
✅ Do say: "Your competitors show up with star ratings and phone numbers in Google. You show up as plain text."

Always connect to: Lost calls, lost customers, competitors winning
⚡ Common Issues Translation:
• Thin content → "Your site only has 150 words. Google needs 400+ to rank pages."
• Not mobile optimized → "60% of searches are on phones. Your site doesn't work on mobile."
• No address visible → "Google won't rank you for 'plumber near me' without seeing your address. That's 70% of local searches."
• No phone number → "People want to call NOW. They can't find your number in 3 seconds, they're gone."
🔄 THE TRANSITION (10 seconds)
"Look, I know you're busy running a plumbing business, not worrying about websites. That's exactly why I'm calling — this stuff is my job, not yours."
💡 Why this works: Acknowledges they're busy. Shows empathy. Positions you as the expert who handles this FOR them.
🎁 THE OFFER (15 seconds)
"What I'd like to do is send you a free breakdown of what I found. It's a 5-minute read, shows you exactly what's wrong and how to fix it. Completely free, no strings attached. I'm not going to call you back trying to sell you something."
💡 Why this works: "Free" said three times. "5 minutes" = small commitment. "No strings" removes pressure. "Not going to call back" = huge relief.
🤝 THE CLOSE (10 seconds)
"If that sounds useful, what's the best email address to send it to?"
💡 Why this works: Assumes the sale ("if that sounds useful" not "would you like"). Asking for email is softer than booking a meeting.
🛡️ Common Objections
"I'm too busy"
"Totally understand — you're running a business! Can I grab 90 seconds now to explain what I found? If it sounds useful I'll email details. If not, no worries."
"We already have a website"
"Yeah absolutely — I saw it. The issue isn't that you don't have a site, it's that Google isn't ranking it. Having a website nobody can find is like having a billboard in your basement."
"How much?"
"Great question — but I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call. I just want to send you the free breakdown. Once you've read it, if you want to chat about costs, we can."
"Send me an email"
"Perfect, I will — what's your email? Also, just so you know what to expect: it'll be a short PDF showing the 3 main things Google doesn't like about your site, and rough costs to fix each. That's it."
